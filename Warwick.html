Overview and History

Warwick is a city in Kent County, Rhode Island, and the third-largest in the state, with a population of 82,823, incorportated in April 21, 1931, as of the 2020 census. Located 12 miles south of Providence, Rhode Island, Warwick was founded in 1642 by Samuel Gorton and has significant historical relevance, including events like King Philip's War and the Gaspee Affair, a precursor to the American Revolution. It was home to notable military figures Nathanael Greene and George S. Greene. Today, Warwick houses T. F. Green Airport, serving the Providence area and as an alternate to Boston's Logan Airport, and features Rocky Point, now a state park.

(Source: Wikipedia )

City Information

The city of Warwick, Rhode Island, has a median income of $81,009, in comparison to the average state income of $81,370. Warwick, Rhode Island, is considered to be a suburban city. Warwick is located in the Kent County region of the state. With a population of approximately 82,823 residents, it is the second-largest city in the state. Warwick is known for its rich history, including being the site of the first shot fired in the American Revolution against the British schooner Gaspee in 1772. The city boasts a variety of recreational activities, with over 39 miles of coastline, several beaches, parks, and marinas. Warwick also has a diverse range of shopping centers, restaurants, and cultural venues, making it a vibrant community for both residents and visitors..
