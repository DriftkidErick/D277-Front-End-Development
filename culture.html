Things To Do In Rhode Island

Check out the Zoo

Roger Williams Park and Zoo, located in Providence, Rhode Island, is a premier family destination known for its diverse animal exhibits and picturesque landscapes. The zoo, established in 1872, is one of the oldest in the country and spans over 40 acres within the larger 435-acre Roger Williams Park. Visitors can explore various themed areas housing over 160 species, including rare and endangered animals. The park also features beautiful botanical gardens, serene lakes, and walking paths. Educational programs, conservation efforts, and seasonal events like the Jack-O-Lantern Spectacular make it a dynamic and engaging attraction for all ages.

Great Food

Rhode Island's culinary scene is a rich blend of coastal and cultural influences. Famous for its seafood, the state offers iconic dishes like clear-broth clam chowder, stuffed clams (stuffies), and spicy Rhode Island-style calamari. Local favorites include Del's Lemonade, hot wieners from Olneyville New York System, doughboys, coffee milk, Johnnycakes, and bakery pizza strips. These diverse and delicious offerings provide a unique taste of New England's traditions.

Nice Views

Rhode Island, though small, offers diverse tourist attractions. Newport is famous for its Gilded Age mansions and scenic Cliff Walk. Providence features the vibrant WaterFire installation and the historic Federal Hill neighborhood. Block Island provides pristine beaches and charming small-town vibes. History enthusiasts can explore the Roger Williams National Memorial and the Slater Mill Historic Site. Outdoor activities abound in state parks and beaches like Colt State Park and Misquamicut State Beach. The RISD Museum in Providence and the Newport Folk and Jazz Festivals also draw many visitors.
