Newport, Rhode Island

Overview and History

Newport, a seaside town on Aquidneck Island in Rhode Island, USA, sits in Narragansett Bay. It's about 33 miles southeast of Providence, 20 miles south of Fall River, MA, 74 miles south of Boston, and 180 miles northeast of New York City. Known for being a summer hotspot in New England, Newport is famous for its grand mansions and sailing heritage. Incorporated in 1784, around 25,000 people call it home. Newport is located in the southeastern region of Rhode Island.

(Source: Wikipedia)

City Information

Newport, Rhode Island, has a population of around 25,000 people, with a median household income of $81,330. It's located along the scenic New England coast and is known for its suburban atmosphere, blending historic charm with coastal beauty. The downtown area features well-preserved architecture, bustling waterfront activities, and a variety of shops and restaurants. Beyond downtown, Newport offers peaceful residential neighborhoods with tree-lined streets and green spaces, making it a desirable suburban destination with a coastal flair."

(Source: Census & Britannica )
